Hi — I’m Fairoz Khan

Student • interested in systems, data, and how “neutral” tools affect real people

I’m taking LIS 500 because I want to understand the “power layer” behind technology—who gets represented, who gets excluded, and how design choices quietly become social rules. I’m interested in data and backend systems, so it’s useful (and honestly kind of uncomfortable sometimes) to see how “efficiency” can also mean surveillance, unequal access, or unfair outcomes.

Outside of class, I’m drawn to building projects that organize information (dashboards, pipelines, APIs), but this course pushes me to ask: what assumptions are hiding in the data? who is missing? and what happens when errors hit the same groups repeatedly?

What I’m trying to learn

  • How bias can be built into tools through data, labeling, and defaults
  • Why intersectionality matters (race/gender/class aren’t separable in practice)
  • How to communicate these ideas clearly without “tech jargon”

Where to go next

If you’re new to these topics, the Implicit Bias Resources page is written like a “subject guide” for first-year students. The Tech Hero page is a short profile showing how access and equity can be advanced through community + education work.
