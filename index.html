Claim

Power shows up in system defaults: what gets ranked, flagged, funded, surveilled, or ignored. “Neutral” choices often have non-neutral outcomes.

So what?

Intersectionality changes the analysis: it asks who bears the costs of “efficiency,” who is made legible to institutions, and who gets meaningful recourse when systems fail.
