Welcome

This website is part of LIS 500: Code and Power at UW–Madison. Our goal is to think critically about how technology intersects with race, identity, labor, and power — and how “neutral” systems can still produce unequal outcomes.

Throughout this site, we connect course concepts (like intersectionality, bias, and visibility) to real examples from tech and digital culture.

About Us

Created by: Fairoz Khan, Teammate Name 2, Teammate Name 3 | UW–Madison

We’re focusing on how power shows up in everyday tech decisions — who gets represented, who gets ignored, and who takes the risk when systems fail. We’re also paying attention to how “diversity” is sometimes framed in a way that still leaves people out.

What we’re analyzing

How platforms and institutions make people “legible” through data, categories, and assumptions — and what happens when those assumptions don’t fit real lives.

Why intersectionality matters

It pushes us to look at overlapping identities (race, gender, class, etc.) instead of treating inequality like a one-variable problem.
