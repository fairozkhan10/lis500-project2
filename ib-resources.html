Key terms (in plain language)

Implicit bias is when we make quick associations or judgments without realizing it. It’s not always intentional, but it can still affect real decisions.

Intersectionality is a way to understand how parts of identity (race, gender, class, etc.) overlap and shape someone’s experience with power and inequality.

Implicit Bias Test (IBT/IAT) is an online test that measures how fast you associate certain ideas (like “good/bad” or jobs) with different social groups.

How to interpret IBT results

  • The IBT can be useful for self-awareness, especially around snap judgments.
  • It shouldn’t be treated like a “personality score” or proof that someone is a “good/bad” person.
  • Results can be influenced by context (speed, fatigue, familiarity), so it’s better as a learning tool than a label.

Why this matters in tech

Implicit bias becomes a “code & power” issue when assumptions get baked into systems: who gets flagged, who gets ranked higher, who gets trusted, or who gets ignored. Even small choices can scale when they’re automated.
